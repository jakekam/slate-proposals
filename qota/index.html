The Company

A homeowner-first contractor marketplace, ready to launch.

Qota flips the model. Instead of homeowners chasing quotes and pros paying ~$80 a lead, a homeowner posts a project once, nearby pros are notified instantly, and the bids come to them. Pros pay nothing per lead — Qota earns through a small convenience fee and an optional Featured Pro tier, with funds held in escrow until the job is done so homeowners are protected end to end.

Built from the ground up by founder Moy Bemaras, Qota is at the launch line: the app is built and a pro pipeline is ready, but the brand has no public presence yet. This campaign exists to change that — to make Qota look and feel like a real, trusted, national company from day one, and to build the advertising engine that turns that presence into signups.

Where Qota Is Now

What we're solving for, in priority order.

1

No public brand presenceHighest priority

The app is built and pros are lined up, but there's no Instagram, no content, nothing that signals "real company." Before Qota can court influencers or run ads, it needs a legit presence for them to point to. This is the single biggest blocker to everything else.

2

Pre-launch means no real jobs to document — yet

With Qota not live, there's no existing job footage or testimonials. That's fine for launch: early content tells the story of what Qota does — relatable scripted scenarios, UGC, AI/stock-style visuals — rather than documenting real jobs. Once live, every job becomes future content.

3

No marketing playbook

You're sharp on the product and the business model but don't have a marketing plan — and you want a partner who builds the strategy, not one who hands you homework. The plan itself is a core deliverable here.

4

A make-or-break launch window

By your own framing, the first stretch makes or breaks the concept. The campaign hits hard and fast, builds a strong foundation, then reassesses — rather than committing to a long, slow burn before the concept is proven.

Our Approach

The 8-Week Launch Foundation.

A strategy-led starter campaign built for a brand at launch stage. Instead of posting random content on day one, we spend the first phase building the foundation — strategy, brand presence, a content bank, and the lead-gen advertising engine — then go live with momentum.

≈ Weeks 1–6

Phase 1 — Foundation & Content Build

  • Lock brand strategy, content pillars, and the full 8-week roadmap
  • Build & optimize the social profiles (Instagram-first, plus TikTok & Facebook)
  • Cast 3 vouching creators and produce 9 UGC videos, 10 social-styled video ads + 10 owner-filmed edits
  • Direct & edit founder content; produce the 60-second animated explainer
  • Stand up the lead-gen advertising system — Google/YouTube + Meta/IG: tracking, audiences, and creative
  • Meanwhile, you onboard pros so supply is ready when homeowner demand arrives
≈ Weeks 6–8

Phase 2 — Launch & Amplify

  • First posts go live; consistent posting cadence begins
  • Google/YouTube + Meta/IG campaigns go live; boosted & paid social behind top performers
  • Activate influencer & paid distribution
  • Track performance and report on reach, signups, and cost per result
End of Week 8

Reassess & Continue

  • Review the data together and decide the next move — most clients continue on a monthly retainer
  • Scale what's working; layer in add-ons (more ad spend, influencers, OOH, NY/NJ) as the concept proves out
  • Deliberately not over-committing before the concept is proven — that protects your budget

Cascade scaling

We don't dump the budget on day one. Spend starts slow across a spread of creatives and audiences, then we review performance every week and cascade budget into the highest-performing ads, platforms, and audiences — scaling winners up and cutting what isn't converting. By the back half of the campaign, the spend is concentrated behind proven performers.

Built to continue.

The 8-week campaign is the on-ramp, not the finish line. It's structured to flow straight into an optional monthly retainer once the concept is proven — we reassess at week 8 and scale what's working (content volume, ad management, new cities) without rebuilding from scratch.
